The problem

37 products. Zero guidance.

Shell Water Systems sells excellent water filtration products — but their shop presents all 37 SKUs to every visitor with no guidance on which to choose. Customers who don't know the difference between a water softener, a salt-free conditioner, and a whole-house filter (which is most of them) freeze up and leave.

⚠ Current state
Customers arrive at the shop page, face an overwhelming product grid, can't identify what they need, and abandon — at a 90% rate. The product quality isn't the issue. The buying experience is.
✓ Solution
A short guided quiz that speaks the customer's language — water problems they recognize — and ends with a single confident recommendation and a direct link to buy it.
The quiz

5 questions, one answer

Each question is designed to eliminate products that don't fit and zero in on the right system. The order matters — water source comes first because well water immediately changes the product set, saving customers from irrelevant options.

1
City or well water?
Well water routes directly to the H2S/Iron unit — the only system built for untreated groundwater.
2
What problems are you experiencing?
Multi-select. Hard water + taste = combo system. Hard water only = softener. Taste only = whole-house filter.
3
Do you want filtered drinking water at the tap?
If yes, the RO system is added as a secondary recommendation on the results screen.
4
How many bathrooms?
Determines sizing (1–3, 4–6, 7+). Passed through to the product page where variants are pre-selected.
5
Salt-based or salt-free preference?
Splits softener/combo recommendations between salt and salt-free product lines. "Not sure" defaults to salt-based (most effective).

Deployment

Two ways to go live

Option A — JotForm HTML Widget
Open shellwater-quiz-embed.html in a text editor. Copy everything between the <body> tags. In JotForm, drag in a Custom HTML widget and paste it in. The quiz renders fully inside JotForm.

Option B — Standalone page (recommended)
Upload the full shellwater-quiz-embed.html file to the Shell Water server as shellwatersystems.com/find-my-system. Link to it from the homepage hero, the top nav, and any JotForm thank-you redirects. This gives more control over layout and avoids JotForm widget size limitations.
